An Opera of Tragic Beauty

The Story

"Madame Butterfly" is a tale of love and betrayal, set against the backdrop of Japan's serene landscapes and vibrant culture. The story revolves around Cio-Cio-san, a young Japanese geisha, whose unwavering devotion to an American naval officer leads her to a heartrending fate.

The Composer

Giacomo Puccini, one of the most celebrated opera composers, brought "Madame Butterfly" to life with his signature lush melodies and emotional depth. His music paints an evocative picture of love's passion and despair.

The Legacy

Since its debut in 1904, "Madame Butterfly" has captured the hearts of audiences worldwide. Its themes of cultural collision and enduring love resonate deeply, making it one of the most performed operas of all time.
